# Chapter 2 — What Is Money?

Most people think money is wealth.

It isn't.

Money is simply a common language for exchanging value.

Instead of saying

```
3kg Rice

=

1 Pair of Shoes
```

We say

```
₹500
```

Money is only the middleman.

```
Rice

↓

Money

↓

Shoes
```

---

## Definition

### Money

A medium of exchange.

It allows people to exchange value without directly exchanging goods.

# Chapter 4 — Why Does Gold Have Value?

Imagine people hear

```
War might happen.

Banks might fail.

Government is printing money.
```

People become afraid.

They think

> I don't trust paper money anymore.

Suppose

```
100 People

Only

10 Gold Bars
```

Everyone tries to buy gold.

```
Demand ↑

Supply stays same

↓

Gold Price ↑
```

Gold itself didn't change.

Human trust changed.

---

## `Why Gold?`

Gold

`✔ Cannot be printed`

`✔ Doesn't rust`

`✔ Is difficult to mine`

`✔ Has been trusted for thousands of years`

That is why it is called a

## `Safe Haven Asset`

---

## Definition

### Safe Haven Asset

An asset people trust during uncertain times.

Examples

- Gold
- Sometimes government bonds of financially strong countries

# Chapter 11 — What If Every Bank Runs Out Of Dollars?

Now RBI enters.

Imagine RBI as India's giant emergency water tank.

```
                 RBI

          +----------------+

          | ₹  $  €  Gold |

          +----------------+

             /   |    \

            /    |     \

         SBI  HDFC  Kotak
```

As of the latest available data in early July 2026, India’s foreign exchange reserves (held by the RBI) stand at around **$674.2 billion**. [tradingeconomics](https://tradingeconomics.com/india/foreign-exchange-reserves)

## Recent trend

So reserves have been broadly in the **$670–700 billion** band in 2026, with some weekly volatility but still considered “healthy” by RBI standards (comfortably covering ~11 months of imports).

---

# Definition

## Foreign Exchange Reserves

Foreign currencies and reserve assets owned by a country's central bank.

Used for

- International payments
- Stabilizing the currency
- Emergencies

# Key Vocabulary

## Money

A medium of exchange.

---

## Currency

The official money used by a country.

Examples

- INR
- USD
- EUR

---

## Inflation

When money buys fewer goods than before.

---

## Purchasing Power

How much goods and services money can buy.

---

## Safe Haven Asset

An asset trusted during uncertain times.

Example

Gold.

---

## Foreign Exchange (Forex)

The exchange of one currency for another.

---

## Foreign Exchange Reserves

Foreign currencies and reserve assets owned by a central bank.

---

## Commercial Bank

A bank that serves customers.

Examples

- SBI
- HDFC
- Kotak

---

## Central Bank

A bank that manages a country's money and banking system.

In India,

this is RBI.

---

## Bond

A loan given by investors to a government or company.

---

## Default

Failure to repay borrowed money.
