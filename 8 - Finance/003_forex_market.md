# Who Runs the Forex Market?

## Understanding the World's Largest Financial Network from First Principles

> _"The Forex Market has no owner. It works because thousands of organizations around the world cooperate using common rules and technology."_

---

# Introduction

One of the biggest misconceptions is:

> **"There must be one company that owns the Forex Market."**

The answer is:

**No one owns it.**

Just like no one owns the Internet.

Let's understand why.

---

# First Principles

Imagine you send an email.

```text
You

↓

Gmail

↓

Outlook

↓

Your Friend
```

Who owns email?

Google?

❌

Microsoft?

❌

Apple?

❌

Nobody.

Google builds Gmail.

Microsoft builds Outlook.

Apple builds Mail.

All of them follow common rules (called protocols), so they can communicate with each other.

The Forex Market works in exactly the same way.

---

# What is the Forex Market?

The **Foreign Exchange Market (Forex)** is simply a **global network where people and organizations buy and sell currencies.**

For example,

- USD ⇄ INR
- USD ⇄ JPY
- EUR ⇄ USD
- SGD ⇄ INR

Millions of these trades happen every day.

---

# Role of Banks

Suppose Apple India needs dollars.

It doesn't call America.

It simply calls its bank.

```text
Apple India

      │

Need USD

      │

      ▼

Kotak Bank
```

If Kotak has dollars,

it sells them.

If not,

Kotak buys dollars from another bank.

Maybe

```text
Kotak

↓

JP Morgan

↓

HSBC

↓

Citibank
```

Banks trade currencies with each other every second.

They are the backbone of the Forex Market.

# Who Builds the Software?

This is where many software engineers become curious.

Every bank builds or buys its own software.

For example,

```text
Kotak

├── Core Banking System

├── Forex Trading System

├── Risk Engine

├── Payment System


JP Morgan

├── Trading Platform

├── Settlement System

├── Compliance System


SBI

├── Core Banking

├── Forex Platform

├── Payment System
```

There is **no single Forex software**.

Thousands of software systems communicate with each other.

---

# How Do Banks Communicate?

Banks use professional trading platforms.

Think of them as Uber,

but instead of matching drivers and passengers,

they match buyers and sellers of currencies.

Some examples include:

- EBS
- LSEG FX (formerly Refinitiv Matching)
- Bloomberg FXGO

These platforms allow banks to instantly find someone willing to buy or sell a currency.

---

# How Does Money Actually Move?

After two banks agree on a trade,

the money still has to move.

Banks use secure payment networks.

One famous example is:

```text
SWIFT
```

SWIFT does **not** move money.

It sends secure messages like:

> "Kotak is paying JP Morgan $10 million."

The banks then settle the payment using their own accounts and national payment systems.

---

# Where Does RBI Fit?

Many people think RBI controls the Forex Market.

Not exactly.

Think of a football match.

```text
Players

↓

Play Football

↓

Referee

↓

Ensures Fair Play
```

The referee doesn't score goals.

The players do.

Similarly,

```text
Banks

↓

Trade Currencies

↓

Forex Market

↓

Exchange Rate Changes

↓

RBI

↓

Maintains Stability
```

RBI is a participant in the market,

but a very powerful one because it holds large foreign exchange reserves.

---

# Why Does RBI Intervene?

Suppose everyone suddenly wants dollars.

```text
Huge Demand For USD

↓

Rupee Falls Quickly

↓

Market Becomes Unstable
```

RBI may sell some of its dollar reserves.

```text
RBI

↓

Sells USD

↓

More Dollars Available

↓

Panic Reduces
```

---

Now imagine the opposite.

Everyone suddenly wants rupees.

```text
Huge Demand For INR

↓

Rupee Rises Too Quickly

↓

Exporters Suffer
```

RBI may buy dollars.

```text
Market

↓

USD

↓

RBI

↓

Pays INR
```

This helps reduce excessive volatility.

Notice something important:

RBI does **not** decide what the exchange rate should be.

It simply helps prevent sudden and disorderly movements.

# Key Vocabulary

## Forex Market

The global marketplace where currencies are bought and sold.

---

## Commercial Bank

Banks like SBI, Kotak, HDFC, JP Morgan, and HSBC that execute currency trades for customers.

---

## Central Bank

A country's main bank responsible for financial stability.

Examples:

- RBI (India)
- Federal Reserve (USA)
- Bank of Japan
- European Central Bank

---

## SWIFT

A secure messaging network that allows banks around the world to exchange payment instructions.

It **does not** move money.

It tells banks **how** to move money.

---

## Trading Platform

Electronic systems where banks buy and sell currencies with each other.

The Forex Market is very similar to the Internet.

```text

```

Similarly,

```text
Forex Market

↓

↓

Thousands of Software Systems
```

There is **no single owner**.

No single software.

It is one of the world's largest distributed systems, where thousands of organizations cooperate using common rules, technology, and trust.

As software engineers, you can think of the Forex Market as **a giant distributed system**, where every bank is a node, every currency trade is a message, and central banks act like stability managers that keep the entire network functioning smoothly.
