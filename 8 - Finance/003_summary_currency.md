# Key Vocabulary

## Currency

The official money used by a country.

Examples:

- INR
- USD
- SGD
- EUR

---

## Exchange Rate

The price of one currency in terms of another.

Example:

```
$1 = вВє86
```

---

## Forex Market

The marketplace where currencies are bought and sold.

---

## Demand

The desire to buy something.

Higher demand generally pushes prices higher.

---

## Supply

The amount available to sell.

Higher supply generally puts downward pressure on prices.

---

## Foreign Exchange Reserves

Foreign currencies and reserve assets held by a country's central bank.

India's reserves help RBI stabilize the currency during periods of excessive volatility.

---

## Central Bank

A country's main bank responsible for maintaining monetary and financial stability.

In India,

this is the Reserve Bank of India (RBI).

---

# Final Thought

No single person controls the value of the rupee.

Not the Prime Minister.

Not RBI.

Not banks.

Every day,

millions of exporters, importers, tourists, investors, companies, students, workers abroad, and banks make decisions that either create demand for the rupee or demand for foreign currencies.

The Forex market combines all of those decisions into a single number:

```
$1 = вВє86
```

RBI's job is **not to choose that number**.

Its job is to make sure that fear, panic, or speculation do not cause sudden and disorderly movements that could harm businesses, households, and the broader economy.

In simple words:

> **The market decides the direction. RBI helps keep the journey stable.**
