# PART 1 — WHY CALCULUS EXISTS

Understanding why humanity invented calculus.

---

## 10.1 The Problem of Change

### Central Question

How do we mathematically describe things that continuously change?

Examples:

```text
Speed changing
Temperature changing
Population changing
Stock prices changing
Neural network weights changing
```

Arithmetic cannot solve this.

Geometry cannot solve this.

A new mathematical framework was needed.

---

## 10.2 The Limits of Geometry

Geometry describes:

```text
Shapes
Distances
Areas
Volumes
```

But geometry struggles with:

```text
Motion
Acceleration
Growth
Flow
```

---

## 10.3 The Limits of Algebra

Algebra describes relationships.

But cannot easily answer:

```text
How fast is something changing right now?
```

---

## 10.4 The Birth of Calculus

Historical Motivation:

- Astronomy
- Physics
- Engineering
- Navigation

Key figures:

- Isaac Newton
- Gottfried Leibniz

Problems they wanted to solve:

- Planetary motion
- Gravity
- Velocity
- Acceleration
