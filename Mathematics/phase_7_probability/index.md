# PART 1 — WHY PROBABILITY EXISTS

Understanding why humanity invented probability.

---

## 7.1 Life Before Probability

### Central Question

How do you make decisions when you do not know the future?

Every civilization faced uncertainty:

- Weather
- Crops
- Disease
- Hunting
- Trade
- War
- Navigation

Humans needed a framework for reasoning about uncertainty.

---

## 7.2 Deterministic Reality vs Uncertain Reality

Some systems are predictable.

Examples:

```text
2 + 2 = 4

Water freezes at 0°C

A compiler given identical code produces identical output
```

Other systems are uncertain.

Examples:

```text
Will it rain tomorrow?

Will the stock go up?

Will a startup succeed?

Will a user click a button?
```

Probability was invented to reason about uncertain systems.

---

## 7.3 The Birth of Probability

Historical origins:

- Gambling
- Dice
- Card games
- Insurance
- Trade

Questions people wanted answered:

```text
What is the chance of winning?

What is the risk of losing?

How much should I charge for insurance?

How likely is a ship to sink?
```

---

## 7.4 What Is Probability?

### Definition

```text
Probability
=
A numerical representation of uncertainty
```

Probability measures:

```text
How likely an event is to occur
```

---

## 7.5 Why Probability Changed Civilization

Without probability:

- No insurance
- No modern finance
- No weather forecasting
- No epidemiology
- No machine learning
- No scientific experimentation

# PART 2 — OUTCOMES AND EVENTS

The building blocks of uncertainty.

---

## 7.6 What Is An Experiment?

An experiment is a process whose outcome is uncertain.

Examples:

- Tossing a coin
- Rolling a die
- Launching a startup
- Running a machine learning model

---

## 7.7 Outcomes

An outcome is a possible result.

Coin toss:

```text
Heads
Tails
```

Die roll:

```text
1
2
3
4
5
6
```

---

## 7.8 Sample Space

The collection of all possible outcomes.

Examples:

Coin:

```text
{Heads, Tails}
```

Die:

```text
{1,2,3,4,5,6}
```

---

## 7.9 Events

An event is a set of outcomes.

Example:

```text
Rolling an even number
```

Event:

```text
{2,4,6}
```

---

## 7.10 Impossible Events

Events that can never happen.

Examples:

```text
Rolling a 7 on a six-sided die
```

---

## 7.11 Certain Events

Events guaranteed to happen.

Examples:

```text
Rolling a number between 1 and 6
```

---

## 7.12 Event Modeling

Representing uncertainty mathematically.

Using:

- Sets
- Events
