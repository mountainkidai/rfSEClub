# Phase 02 — Observation & Truth

# Introduction

In Phase 00 we learned:

```text
Current State
↓
Desired State
↓
Gap
↓
Problem
```

In Phase 01 we learned:

```text
Problems
↓
Categories
↓
Constraints
↓
Frameworks
```

But before we can define a problem...

Before we can classify a problem...

We must first answer a more fundamental question:

> How do we know something is true?

---

Most people do not observe reality.

They observe:

```text
Opinions

Beliefs

Assumptions

Stories

Emotions
```

and mistake them for reality.

---

Engineers do something different.

They ask:

```text
What is actually happening?
```

not

```text
What do I want to be happening?
```

---

This sounds simple.

It is not.

Entire companies fail because they misread reality.

Entire governments fail because they misread reality.

Entire civilizations collapse because they misread reality.

---

Truth is the foundation of engineering.

Without truth:

```text
Bad Observations
↓
Bad Models
↓
Bad Decisions
↓
Bad Outcomes
```

With truth:

```text
Accurate Observations
↓
Accurate Models
↓
Accurate Decisions
↓
Better Outcomes
```

---

The purpose of this phase is to learn:

```text
Reality
↓
Observation
↓
Evidence
↓
Truth
↓
Knowledge
```

because engineers cannot solve reality if they cannot see reality.
