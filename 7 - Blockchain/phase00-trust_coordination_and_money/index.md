# Phase 0 — Trust, Coordination & Money

## Introduction

Most blockchain courses start with:

```text
Bitcoin
↓
Ethereum
↓
Smart Contracts
↓
Tokens
```

This is backwards.

Blockchain is not fundamentally about:

```text
Coins
Tokens
Mining
NFTs
```

Blockchain exists because humanity has a much older problem:

```text
Trust
```

Every civilization faces the same challenge:

```text
Strangers
↓
Need To Cooperate
↓
Need To Coordinate
↓
Need To Exchange Value
↓
Need To Make Decisions
↓
Need To Trust
```

Historically, trust was provided by:

```text
Kings
Governments
Banks
Courts
Corporations
Religious Institutions
```

These institutions act as:

```text
Trust Machines
```

Blockchain proposes something radical:

```text
Trust
↓
Can Be Engineered
↓
Using Mathematics
Cryptography
Economics
Consensus
```

Before studying Bitcoin, Ethereum, or Koel, you must understand:

```text
Humans
↓
Trust
↓
Coordination
↓
Records
↓
Money
↓
Institutions
↓
Incentives
↓
Consensus
↓
Blockchain
```

The goal of this phase is to understand why blockchains exist at all.
