# Koel Protocol

## The Trust Layer For Civilization

---

## Introduction

Human civilization runs on trust.

Every company.

Every government.

Every university.

Every community.

Every market.

Every friendship.

Every civilization.

All ultimately depend on one thing:

```text
Trust
```

---

Yet trust remains primitive.

Today trust is stored in:

```text
Resumes
Degrees
References
Social Networks
Credit Scores
Personal Reputation
```

These systems are fragmented.

They are centralized.

They are difficult to verify.

They are difficult to transfer.

They are easy to manipulate.

---

Money became digital.

Communication became digital.

Knowledge became digital.

Trust did not.

---

Koel exists to solve this problem.

---

## The Fundamental Question

Proof of Work asks:

```text
Who spent energy?
```

Proof of Stake asks:

```text
Who committed capital?
```

Koel asks:

```text
Who earned trust?
```

---

## The Core Thesis

The most valuable asset in civilization is not money.

It is reputation.

---

Money answers:

```text
What do you own?
```

Trust answers:

```text
Who are you?
```

---

Civilizations depend far more on the second question.

A billionaire with no trust cannot coordinate people.

A trusted person with no money often can.

---

Koel treats trust as a first-class primitive.

---
